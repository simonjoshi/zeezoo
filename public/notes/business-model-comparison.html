The Key Finding

For passive income, laundromats win across every metric that matters:

  • Highest survival rate: 92-95% (vs. 50-80% for the others)
  • Lowest owner hours: 5-10 hrs/week, true semi-absentee potential
  • Fastest payback: 3-5 years to recover your cash
  • Recession-proof: People always do laundry, even in downturns
  • Scalable: You can stack 2-3 units and manage them in your spare time

Car washes have better margins (40-60% net vs. 20-35%), but require 5-10x more capital and are more operationally complex. Boba shops have the worst risk profile: highest failure rate, most labor-intensive, fad-exposed, and growth is decelerating.

This analysis is based on public SBA data, IBISWorld industry reports, and franchise disclosure documents. Confidence levels for each metric are included in the full Conductor report.
